width="825" height="196"></p></div><h2><br><br><br><img src="https://cdn.serilum.com/description/nameplate/description.svg" alt="Description" width="838" height="64"></h2><p><strong><span style="font-size:18px">Texture values instead of generation via player-names</span></strong><br>The mod uses texture values to generate the mob heads. This means that there won't be any lag, which player-name generated heads usually cause.<br><br><strong><span style="font-size:18px">Pure Vanilla Resources</span></strong><br><span style="font-size:12px">Because the mod does not add any custom blocks, you may remove the mod and still have the heads previously gathered in-game.</span><br><br><strong><span style="font-size:18px">When does a head drop?</span></strong><br>Whenever the player kills a mob, there is a chance that they drop their head. There are pre-defined chances in the config file, but you may alter them to your liking. It'll be dropped at their death location.<br><br></p><p><strong><span style="font-size:18px">How many mobs are added?</span></strong><br>Every mob has been added. When Minecraft updates, the newly added mobs receive a head drop as well. You can see the entire mob head list above under configurable.<br><br></p><p><strong><span style="font-size:18px">What about Creepers, Skeletons and Zombies?</span></strong><br>By default the standard heads do not drop, because you can collect them via a charged creeper. You can however enable the option within the config file for all three to drop their respective head on death just as any other mob.<br><br></p><p><strong><span style="font-size:18px">Does Looting have an effect?</span></strong><br>The Looting enchant has an effect on the head drop chance.&nbsp;This can be enabled/disabled within the config file. The increased chance is&nbsp;3.5% at level I, 4.5% at level II, and 5.5% at level III and so forth. It also works with looting levels higher than 3.</p><h2><br><br><br><img src="https://cdn.serilum.com/description/nameplate/notes-n-links.svg" alt="Notes and links" width="838" height="64"></h2><p><span style="font-size:24px"><strong>You may freely use this mod in any modpack, as long as the download remains hosted within the CurseForge or Modrinth ecosystem.</strong></span><br><br><span style="font-size:18px"><a style="font-size:18px;color:#008000" href="https://serilum.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Serilum.com</a> contains an overview and more information on all mods available.</span><br><br><span style="font-size:14px">Comments are disabled as I'm unable to keep track of them across so many separate mod pages.</span><span style="font-size:14px"><br>For issues, ideas, suggestions or anything else there is the&nbsp;<a style="font-size:14px;color:#008000" href="https://github.com/Serilum/.issue-tracker" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Github repo</a>.
